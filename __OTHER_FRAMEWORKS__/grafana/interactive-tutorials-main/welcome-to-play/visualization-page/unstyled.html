üåç Geographic Data with Geomap

Geomaps bring your location-based data to life, perfect for tracking global services, user distribution, or infrastructure across regions.

  • The Geomap visualization displays data on interactive maps using various layers like markers, heatmaps, and routes. It's perfect for visualizing geographic distributions, tracking service health across regions, monitoring IoT devices by location, or analyzing user traffic patterns. Geomap supports multiple map layers including OpenStreetMap, CARTO, and custom tile servers, and can display data as circles, heatmaps, or custom markers with dynamic sizing and coloring based on metric values. Explore the Geomap visualization for geographic and location-based data.

üî• Performance Analysis with Flame Graphs

Flame graphs are essential for performance profiling, helping you identify bottlenecks and optimize system performance at a glance.

  • The Flame Graph visualization is a powerful tool for profiling and performance analysis. It displays hierarchical data where the width of each block represents the amount of time or resources consumed. This visualization is particularly valuable for analyzing CPU profiles, memory allocations, and call stacks. Each level represents a stack frame, and you can quickly identify performance hotspots by looking for wide blocks. Flame graphs are interactive - you can click to zoom into specific code paths and hover for detailed metrics. Discover Flame Graphs for profiling and performance optimization.

üé® Creative Layouts with Canvas

Canvas visualizations give you complete creative freedom to design custom layouts and interactive dashboards beyond traditional panel constraints.

  • The Canvas visualization provides a free-form design surface where you can arrange elements exactly as you envision them. Unlike traditional panels constrained to a grid, Canvas lets you place text, images, shapes, metrics, and icons anywhere on the canvas. It's perfect for creating custom dashboards like network topology diagrams, process flow visualizations, building floor plans with sensor overlays, or branded executive dashboards. You can layer elements, use background images, and create truly unique data presentations that match your specific needs. Check out the Canvas visualization for custom, free-form dashboard designs.

üìà Time-Based Analysis with Time Series

Time series graphs are the foundation of observability, showing how your metrics evolve over time with rich customization options.

  • The Time Series visualization is Grafana's most versatile and widely-used graph type. It displays data points over time with support for multiple series, various drawing modes (lines, bars, points), and advanced features like thresholds, annotations, and custom axes. Time series graphs excel at showing trends, patterns, and anomalies in metrics data. You can customize line styles, fill opacity, stack series, apply transforms, and use features like value mappings and overrides to highlight important data. It's the go-to visualization for monitoring system metrics, application performance, business KPIs, and any time-based data. Explore Time Series visualizations for trend analysis and monitoring.

üéØ Choosing the Right Visualization

Selecting the appropriate visualization is key to effective data communication:

  • Trends over time? Use Time Series or State Timeline
  • Geographic data? Geomap is your friend
  • Performance profiling? Flame Graphs reveal bottlenecks
  • Category comparison? Bar Charts and Pie Charts work great
  • Single metric focus? Stat panels or Gauges stand out
  • Custom layouts? Canvas gives you complete freedom
  • Financial data? Candlestick charts are purpose-built
  • Density patterns? Heatmaps show distributions beautifully

üí° Best Practices

  • Match the viz to the data - Don't force a pie chart when a time series makes more sense
  • Use color purposefully - Colors should communicate meaning, not just decoration
  • Label clearly - Ensure axes, legends, and tooltips are informative
  • Consider your audience - Technical teams may prefer detailed graphs; executives might want simplified views
  • Test interactivity - Make sure tooltips, legends, and drill-downs work as expected
  • Optimize performance - Some visualizations are more resource-intensive than others
